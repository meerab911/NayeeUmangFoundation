Our Vision

To create a society where every individual—regardless of background—is empowered with education, health, dignity, and hope. Nayee Umang Foundation envisions a future where vulnerable and marginalized communities are uplifted through compassion, opportunity, and sustainable support.

About Nayee Umang

Founded in 2010 by visionary philanthropist Noreen Aziel, Nayee Umang Organization believes that every individual deserves equal access to education, healthcare, and opportunities for growth.

Our foundation works closely with local communities and partners to create sustainable initiatives that empower people to lead healthier, more educated, and dignified lives.

We proudly stand with Pakistan's Christian community—supporting schools, clinics, and neighborhood outreach so that every family, regardless of faith, can access hope and opportunity.

Mission

To bridge social gaps by delivering holistic support across education, health, and social welfare.

Values

Compassion, dignity, equality, and sustainable community empowerment.

Under Meerab's leadership, the organization has expanded its reach to 38 districts, established 89 learning centers, and mobilized over 15 health professionals. Her strategic vision combines data-driven planning with compassionate service delivery, ensuring every program creates measurable impact in the communities we serve.

Our Core Focus

Three pillars guide every initiative and program we deliver on the ground.

Support for School Dropout Students

To identify, mentor, and empower school dropout children by providing access to alternative education, skill-based learning, and guidance—helping them rebuild confidence and reintegrate into education or vocational pathways.

Health Assistance for Needy People

To offer basic healthcare support, awareness, and medical aid to underserved individuals and families, ensuring that financial limitations do not stop them from receiving essential health services.

Upliftment of Street-Based Individuals

To improve the lives of street children, daily-wage workers, and vulnerable community members by providing food, emergency support, learning opportunities, and pathways toward stability and dignity.
